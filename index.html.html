Services

How I help

Every system that moves toward authorization needs someone who can merge technical details and compliance narratives. This is a skill I have experience with — translating scan results, control statements, and architecture diagrams into a clear picture an assessor, an auditor, or an executive can actually act on. That means integrating security throughout the full software development lifecycle. Below is how that shows up across a typical engagement, from the first risk conversation through continuous monitoring after deployment.

01
Security Assessment & Authorization
Guide systems through the full ATO lifecycle — drafting and refining SSPs, SARs, and POA&Ms, and writing control implementation statements that hold up to assessor scrutiny under NIST SP 800-53A.
02
Continuous Monitoring & Compliance
Keep authorized systems audit-ready year-round with ongoing NIST/FedRAMP control validation, evidence collection, and coordinated remediation tracking so nothing is scrambled at reassessment time.
03
Cloud Security & Risk Management
Review and harden AWS and Azure environments — IAM policy design, encryption and access-control validation, and vulnerability remediation tracking across Fortify, WebInspect, and Nessus scan results.
04
Incident Response & SIEM
Tune SIEM detections to cut through alert noise, enforce RBAC and IAM policy at the identity layer, and build dashboards that give stakeholders real visibility instead of a wall of raw logs.
05
AI-Assisted Compliance Workflows
Use tools like Microsoft Copilot to accelerate control mapping, evidence gathering, and documentation drafting — freeing up time for the judgment calls that still need a human in the loop.
